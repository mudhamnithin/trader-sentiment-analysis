Trader Performance vs Market Sentiment Analysis
Objective

Analyze how market sentiment (Fear vs Greed) impacts trader behavior and profitability on Hyperliquid.

Methodology

Cleaned and standardized datasets

Converted timestamps to daily level

Merged trader data with sentiment data

Calculated daily PnL per trader

Computed win rate and drawdown proxy

Analyzed trade frequency, position size, and long/short bias

Created trader segments (frequent vs infrequent, consistent vs inconsistent)

Key Insights

Traders show higher average daily PnL and win rates during Greed periods compared to Fear periods.

During Greed regimes, traders increase position sizes and exhibit stronger long bias.

Frequent traders experience higher volatility and underperform during Fear periods, indicating overtrading under stress.

Strategy Recommendations

Reduce position size and trade frequency during Fear regimes to limit downside exposure.

Maintain controlled exposure during Greed periods while implementing position caps to prevent excessive risk.
